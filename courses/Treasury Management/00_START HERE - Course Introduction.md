## Welcome

Treasury Management is the study of how organisations manage their cash, financial risks, and capital. By the end of this course, you will understand how a treasury department operates — from daily liquidity management to managing interest rate risk, foreign exchange exposure, debt, and investment decisions.

Start here. Read this document first. It tells you what's coming and in what order.

---

## What This Course Is About

Every organisation — from Zanaco to Zambeef to the government of Zambia — needs someone managing its money. That's treasury. This course covers:

1. **What treasury does** — its structure, functions, controls, and how it's organised
2. **Working capital** — managing cash, stock, debtors, and creditors
3. **Risk management** — protecting the organisation from interest rate and FX movements
4. **Funding and investment** — how organisations raise money and invest surplus cash
5. **Systems** — the technology that runs modern treasury operations

## Quick Reference — Key Terms (Module 1)

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Treasury | Financial centre of an organisation — manages cash, risk, and capital |
| Liquidity | Ability to meet financial obligations as they fall due |
| Cost Centre | Treasury measured by costs; not expected to generate profit |
| Profit Centre | Treasury expected to generate income from trading/hedging |
| Segregation of Duties | Front office (trading) separated from back office (settlement) |
| STP | Straight-Through Processing — automated transaction processing |
| Centralised Treasury | All treasury functions run from one location |
| Working Capital | Current assets minus current liabilities |
