--- Slide 5 ---
Introduction to Treasury
Treasury department is the financial center of an organization
Key role is safeguarding and stewardship of organization's financial assets and management of liabilities
Treasury staff perform critically daily liquidity management tasks that help ensure the availability of adequate cash resources needed to sustain organization's ongoing operational activities

--- Slide 6 ---
Introduction to Treasury
Responsible for implementing various financial decisions made by management and board
Has important role in risk reduction and mitigation
Structure varies according to factors such as size, nature of business and complexity of organizational structure

--- Slide 7 ---
Key Decisions of the Finance Function
Grouped into three key decisions areas to provide and manage resources:
Investment decision
How resources available are committed to projects
Investment in working capital e.g. stocks and debtors
Internal and external investment and disinvestment

--- Slide 8 ---
Key Decisions of the Finance Function
b) Financing decision
Acquiring optimum finance to meet financial objectives and effective management of fixed and working capital
Finance manager needs good knowledge of sources and cost of funds
Needs to ensure entity has good sound capital structure
Understand difference between profit and cash
Risk and hedging

--- Slide 9 ---
Key Decisions of the Finance Function
c) Dividend policy
How much and how frequently cash can be paid out of profits
Has two elements; amount to be paid out and amount to be retained to support growth i.e retained earnings.
Level and growth of dividends determines companies market value

Cash management - treasurer must ensure that sufficient cash is available for operational needs

Main Functions

--- Slide 14 ---
Key Tasks of Treasury
Strategic- matters concerning capital structure of business and distribution/retention policies, capital raising, assessment of likely returns, dividend decisions etc.
Tactical- management of cash investments and decision as to hedging of currency or interest risk
Operational- e.g. transmission of cash, placing of surplus cash, dealing with banks etc

--- Slide 15 ---
Cost or Profit Centre
Treasury operations can be set up as a cost or profit center
Cost center is common approach, as treasury is regarded as a support function to the organization and is not expected to make a profit
Disadvantage is that management may focus on cost of treasury operations and not the value provided by function

--- Slide 16 ---
Cost or Profit Centre
This may lead to difficulties obtaining appropriate budget and staff
Profit center approach applies to organisations that heavily involved in global finance, trade, or risk management or companies that are financial service providers
Such have extensive dealings in money, capital, FX, interest rate, or commodities market

--- Slide 17 ---
Cost or Profit Centre
These require buying/selling/holding of securities or use of derivatives and other hedging instruments to increase earnings and/or reduce losses
Rationale for profit center is to generate income from either trading operations or from hedging and/or speculative activities

--- Slide 18 ---
Cost or Profit Centre
Advantages of profit centre
Individual business units charged a market rate for the service provided, making operating costs more realistic
Treasurer motivated to provide services as effectively as possible to ensure that profit is made at market rate

--- Slide 19 ---
Cost or Profit Centre
Disadvantage of profit centre
Profit concept is temptation to speculate e.g. swapping funds from currencies expected to depreciate into those expected to appreciate (i.e. shorting)
Management time unduly spent on arguments with business units over charges for services
Additional admin costs may be excessive

--- Slide 20 ---
Treasury Controls
Given large sums of cash involved in many treasury transactions it is important to set controls that ensure transactions are appropriate (RE: Nick Leeson and Barings Bank)
Segregation
Duties must be segregated among treasury staff, i.e. between front office and back office
E.g. trading activities must be separated from confirmation activities

--- Slide 21 ---
Treasury Controls
2. Delegation of authority
Treasury staff require a sufficient level of autonomy
Delegation ensures that decision-making and power is in the right hands
3. Limits
Treasurer should impose limit controls on a variety of transactions
Limits can prohibit or severely restrict treasury staff from investing in certain types of financial instruments that present an unduly high risk of capital loss

--- Slide 22 ---
Treasury Controls
4. Approvals
Senior level treasury manager should approve all trades, another person should reconcile and account for all transactions
5. Internal audits should be scheduled in order to match actual transactions against company policies and procedures
6. Automation / STP (Straight-Through Processing)

--- Slide 23 ---
Treasury Centralization
A company that operates geographically dispersed sales offices with limited personnel may centralize its treasury functions at the HQ
MNC may centralize all treasury operations in one country
Centralized treasury offers the following advantages:

--- Slide 24 ---
Treasury Centralization
Stronger controls
Economies of scale
Lower operating costs
Tax advantages for MNCs
Centralized treasury grants less autonomy to field office personnel or subsidiaries

--- Slide 25 ---
Treasury Centralization
A company with autonomous subsidiaries and/or multiple operational entities may operate with a decentralized treasury structure
They will use guidelines and/or policies established by the company headquarters
Local subsidiary personnel can offer the advantages of possessing familiarity with local business and banking practice

--- Slide 26 ---
Treasury Centralization
Local staff have intimate knowledge of languages, customs, and cultures
Decentralized structure may result in duplication of effort and resources across units

--- Slide 27 ---
Treasury Centralization
Some large MNCs operate regional treasury centers that combine aspects of centralized and decentralized systems
i.e. while each region is centralized, overall treasury operations are decentralized to regional centers

--- Slide 28-29 ---
Treasury Centralization (Hybrid)
The advent of enterprise-wide accounting and information systems combined with the growth of automated treasury management systems has resulted in the introduction of hybrid centralized treasury systems with decentralized or distributed input and output
Local personnel enter local information into a common treasury management system; centralized treasury staff manages overall operations
