## RESEARCH THEMES TO INVESTIGATE (Chapter 2)

Research the following four themes thoroughly. Each theme needs 3–4 sub-topics with cited academic evidence:

### Theme 1: AI-Powered Learning Technologies in Higher Education
- What are AI-powered learning technologies? (adaptive learning systems, intelligent tutoring systems, AI-driven platforms like ChatGPT, Coursera AI, etc.)
- How are they being used in higher education globally?
- Evidence of their impact on student learning outcomes
- Specific examples in accounting and finance education

### Theme 2: Factors Influencing Technology Adoption
- Technology Acceptance Model (TAM) — Davis (1989) — perceived usefulness and perceived ease of use
- Unified Theory of Acceptance and Use of Technology (UTAUT) — Venkatesh et al. (2003)
- Individual factors: digital literacy, prior tech experience, self-efficacy, attitude
- Institutional factors: infrastructure, lecturer support, internet access, institutional policy
- Social factors: peer influence, subjective norms

### Theme 3: Technology Adoption in Sub-Saharan Africa / Zambia Context
- State of digital infrastructure in Zambian higher education (UNZA, ZCAS, CBU, Mulungushi)
- Barriers to EdTech adoption in Sub-Saharan Africa (connectivity, cost, electricity, device access)
- Existing evidence on technology use by students in Zambian universities
- Government policy on ICT in education in Zambia (e-learning policies, Smart Zambia)

### Theme 4: AI Adoption Among Accounting and Finance Students Specifically
- Why accounting/finance students are a distinct population for this study
- AI tools relevant to accounting/finance education (automated grading, finance simulations, AI tutors)
- Attitudes of business/finance students toward AI tools vs. other disciplines
- Academic integrity concerns related to AI in finance/accounting education

## METHODOLOGY DECISIONS (Chapter 3)

Use these as the methodological stance for the study:

- **Research Paradigm:** Positivist (quantitative, objective, deductive)
- **Research Approach:** Deductive — testing TAM constructs against the student population
- **Research Strategy:** Survey strategy
- **Time Horizon:** Cross-sectional
- **Research Design:** Descriptive-correlational
- **Target Population:** Accounting and finance students at selected higher learning institutions in Zambia (ZCAS University and UNZA are the primary sites)
- **Sampling Technique:** Stratified random sampling
- **Sample Size:** Justify using Slovin's formula or Krejcie & Morgan (1970) table — estimate around 100–150 respondents
- **Data Collection Instrument:** Structured questionnaire (5-point Likert scale, 1 = Strongly Disagree to 5 = Strongly Agree)
- **Data Analysis:** Descriptive statistics + multiple regression analysis (same approach as comparable dissertations at this institution)
- **Reliability:** Cronbach's Alpha (target α > 0.7)
- **Validity:** Content validity (expert review) + construct validity (factor analysis)
